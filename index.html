Mahatma Gandhi

Embracing Nonviolence, Inspiring Change

Here's a time line of Mahatma gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - October 2: Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi is born in Porbandar, India.
  • 1888 - Gandhi travels to London to study law.
  • 1893 - Gandhi goes to South Africa to work as a lawyer and becomes involved in the civil rights movement for Indians in South Africa.
  • 1915 - Gandhi returns to India and becomes a leader in the Indian National Congress.
  • 1919 - The Jallianwala Bagh Massacre occurs, leading to widespread protests against British rule. Gandhi begins organizing nonviolent protests.
  • 1930 - March-April: Gandhi leads the Salt March (Dandi March) to protest the British salt tax, becoming a symbol of nonviolent resistance.
  • 1942 - Gandhi launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule in India.
  • 1947 - August 15: India gains independence from British rule, and Gandhi's efforts in the independence movement are realized.
  • 1948 - January 30: Mahatma Gandhi is assassinated in New Delhi by Nathuram Godse, a Hindu nationalist.

"Mahatma Gandhi, the soul of India's struggle for freedom, was a man of indomitable spirit and unyielding dedication to the principles of truth and nonviolence. He illuminated the path to liberty with the beacon of his unwavering resolve and compassion. His life was a testament to the transformative power of love, tolerance, and civil disobedience. Gandhi's legacy is not just the story of a nation's fight for independence; it is a universal message of hope and the triumph of the human spirit. His profound influence continues to inspire generations, igniting the flame of justice and peace across the world."

--India's First Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru
